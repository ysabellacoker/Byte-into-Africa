Jamaica

Jamaican cuisine blends African, Caribbean, and European influences, creating vibrant flavors and rich dishes. With an abundance of tropical ingredients, Jamaican appetizers are known for their spiciness, boldness, and unique flavor combinations. From crispy patties to savory fritters, each bite reflects the island's rich heritage.

Appetizers

Start your taste adventure with these savory bites, packed with spices and island flavors.

Jamaican Beef Patty

The Jamaican beef patty is a flaky, golden pastry filled with flavorful ground beef seasoned with herbs, spices, and bold seasonings. It’s a popular street food in Jamaica and beyond, known for its vibrant yellow crust and spicy filling.

Ingredients

  • 450g ground beef
  • 2 tsp garlic powder
  • 2 tsp allspice
  • 1 tsp black pepper
  • 2 tsp salt
  • 2 tsp paprika
  • 1 tbsp dried thyme
  • 2 tsp sugar
  • ½ cup beef stock
  • 1 tbsp dark soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp regular soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp browning seasoning
  • 1–2 scotch bonnets
  • 1 small yellow onion
  • 2–3 scallions

Pastry

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 2 tsp turmeric
  • 2 tsp curry powder
  • 100g butter (plus more for layering)
  • 50g lard
  • 125ml cold water

Instructions

  1. Blend onion, scallions, scotch bonnet, and wet seasonings until smooth.
  2. In a hot pan, cook the ground beef with all dry spices until browned.
  3. Add the blended mixture and simmer for 10 minutes, until thickened. Set aside to cool.
  4. In a bowl, combine flour, salt, sugar, turmeric, and curry powder. Grate in cold butter and mix in lard.
  5. Add water and knead into a dough. Chill for 30 minutes.
  6. Roll out dough, layer with grated butter, fold, and roll again. Cut into 7-inch circles.
  7. Fill each with ⅓ cup beef, fold, and seal the edges. Use a fork if desired for crimping.
  8. Bake at 375°F for 25 minutes until golden brown. Serve warm.

Coco Bread

Coco bread is a soft, slightly sweet Caribbean bread made with coconut milk and butter. Its golden crust and pillowy inside make it the perfect side for savory dishes or a delicious snack on its own.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup coconut milk
  • 2–3 tbsp sugar
  • 4 tbsp unsalted butter (plus more for brushing)
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 2¼ tsp instant yeast
  • 1 large egg
  • 3½–4 cups all-purpose flour

Instructions

  1. Warm coconut milk, sugar, salt, and butter until melted (do not boil). Let cool slightly, then add yeast and egg.
  2. Gradually mix in flour until a soft dough forms. Knead on a floured surface for 3–4 minutes.
  3. Place dough in a greased bowl, cover, and let rise for 1–2 hours or until doubled.
  4. Divide into 10 equal pieces, roll each into a 6–7 inch circle.
  5. Brush with melted butter, fold in half, and brush again.
  6. Place on a lined baking sheet and let rest for 10–15 minutes.
  7. Bake at 350°F for 15–20 minutes, until lightly browned. Brush with more butter and serve warm.

Stamp and Go (Saltfish Fritters)

Stamp and Go is a classic Jamaican fritter made with salted cod and spices, fried until crispy and golden. These savory treats are perfect for breakfast or as a snack and are often served with a spicy dipping sauce.

Ingredients

  • ¼ lb salted codfish or pollock
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 3 stalks scallion, chopped
  • ¼ tsp thyme
  • ¼ tsp black pepper
  • Salt (to taste)
  • Hot water (as needed)
  • Cooking oil (for frying)

Instructions

  1. Soak fish overnight to reduce saltiness. Remove bones and skin, then flake into small pieces.
  2. In a bowl, sift flour and mix in flaked fish, scallion, thyme, and pepper. Gradually whisk in hot water to make a thick batter.
  3. Heat oil in a skillet about ½ inch deep.
  4. Using a spoon dipped in oil, scoop batter into the hot oil. Fry until golden on both sides.
  5. Drain on a rack or paper towel and serve hot with chili sauce or pepper jelly.

Main Dishes

Indulge in these rich and flavorful main dishes, showcasing the heart of Jamaican cuisine.

Jerk Chicken

"Jerk" cooking refers to the style of cooking originally done in Jamaica. It involves poking meats with holes to allow more flavors to permeate deeply. Over the years, the term has come to represent the spicy rubs, marinades, and sauces that are now iconic.

Ingredients

  • 1 whole chicken (cut up, or use your favorite cuts)
  • 4-6 Scotch Bonnet peppers, chopped
  • 1 small red onion, chopped
  • 4-6 garlic cloves, chopped
  • 4 stalks scallions, ends trimmed
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup vinegar (white or apple cider vinegar)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Juice from 1 large orange (about ¾ cup orange juice)
  • Juice from half a lime
  • 1 tablespoon freshly grated ginger
  • 2 tablespoons brown sugar
  • 1 teaspoon nutmeg
  • 1 teaspoon allspice
  • 1 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme (or fresh if available)
  • Salt and pepper to taste (about 1 teaspoon salt and 1 tablespoon black pepper)

Instructions

  1. For the jerk marinade, add all the ingredients to a food processor or blender and process until smooth.
  2. Poke holes in the chicken with a knife to allow the marinade to penetrate more deeply.
  3. Place the chicken in a large bowl or sealable baggie, pour the marinade over it, and rub it down to coat. Refrigerate and marinate for at least 2 hours (overnight is better). Discard the marinade before cooking.
  4. To grill the chicken, heat a charcoal or gas grill to medium-high heat, lightly oil the grates, and cook the chicken for 12-20 minutes or until the internal temperature reaches 165°F.
  5. To bake the chicken, heat the oven to 400°F. Bake for 35-40 minutes, or until the internal temperature reaches 165°F.
  6. Serve and enjoy!

Oxtails

Oxtail is a beloved Jamaican dish, slow-cooked with spices to create a rich, flavorful stew. The meat is tender and falls off the bone, while the savory gravy is perfect with rice and peas.

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs (907.18 g) Oxtail, chopped into 2-3cm chunks
  • 1 cup Butterbean
  • 1 tbsp Oxtail or all-purpose seasoning
  • 1 tsp Browning
  • 1 tsp Salt
  • 1 ½ tbsp Dark brown sugar
  • 1 Maggi stock cube
  • 1.8 oz (51.03 g) Onion, sliced
  • 2.5 oz (70.87 g) Bell pepper, sliced
  • ½ Scotch bonnet pepper, seeds removed and finely chopped
  • 2.1 oz (59.53 g) Tomato, diced
  • 1.5 oz (42.52 g) Stalks scallion (green onion), chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, crushed
  • 5 sprigs thyme
  • 0.28 oz (7.94 g) Freshly grated ginger
  • 7 Pimento berries (allspice)
  • 2 tbsp Ketchup
  • 3 tbsp Cooking oil
  • Water
  • 1 small lime (to wash the oxtail)
  • 1 tbsp Vinegar (to wash the oxtail)

Instructions

  1. Wash the oxtail in water with vinegar and lime juice. Drain and dry thoroughly.
  2. Season the oxtail with salt, oxtail seasoning, garlic, ginger, browning, and scotch bonnet pepper. Mix well and marinate for at least an hour.
  3. Heat cooking oil and brown sugar in a pressure cooker. Once the oil is hot and the sugar has melted, add the oxtail and sear all sides until browned.
  4. Add 2 ½ cups of water to the pressure cooker. Close and cook at high pressure for 30-35 minutes. Let the pressure release naturally.
  5. Carefully open the pressure cooker, check that the meat is tender, and add onion, thyme, scallion, tomato, bell pepper, butterbean, ketchup, and Maggi stock cube. Simmer for 30 minutes.
  6. Serve with rice and peas.

Curry Goat

Curry goat is a traditional Jamaican dish often served for special occasions. It’s spicy, savory, and full of flavor, making it a favorite in many Jamaican homes.

Ingredients

  • 3-3½ lbs goat meat (cut into chunks)
  • ¼-½ cup cooking oil
  • 2 tsp minced garlic
  • 1-2 tsp minced ginger
  • 1 medium onion, sliced
  • 4-5 tbsp curry powder
  • 1 tsp white pepper
  • 1-2 tsp fresh thyme
  • 2 green onions, sliced
  • 2-3 medium potatoes
  • 1 tbsp tomato paste
  • 1 scotch bonnet pepper (or to taste)
  • 1 tbsp Bouillon powder (optional)
  • Salt to taste

Instructions

  1. Season the goat meat with salt and pepper. Set aside.
  2. In a large pot, heat the oil and sauté the goat meat until browned on all sides.
  3. Add curry powder and stir for about 1-2 minutes. Then add garlic, ginger, white pepper, onions, thyme, tomato paste, scallions, and scotch bonnet pepper. Stir for a minute.
  4. Pour enough water to cover the goat and bring it to a boil. Reduce heat and simmer for 2 hours or until the meat is tender.
  5. 15-20 minutes before finishing, add the potatoes and Bouillon powder. Continue simmering until potatoes are tender and the curry thickens.
  6. Serve with rice or roti.

Desserts and Drinks

Experience the sweet side of Jamaican cuisine with these delicious and traditional desserts.

Gizzada (Pinch Me Around)

Gizzada is a popular Jamaican pastry filled with a sweet, spiced coconut mixture. It's often enjoyed as a snack or dessert, and its name comes from the way the dough is pinched around the filling to form a crust.

Ingredients

Filling

  • 2 cups dried unsweetened shredded coconut (or freshly grated)
  • 1 cup brown sugar
  • ¼ tsp nutmeg
  • ½ tsp cinnamon
  • ¼ tsp ginger powder
  • ½ tsp rose water
  • ½ tbsp vanilla extract
  • ⅛ tsp salt
  • ¼ cup coconut milk (or swap with more water if using freshly grated coconut)
  • ¼ cup water

Crust

  • 1 ½ cups flour
  • ½ cup (1 stick) cold salted butter
  • ⅓ cup ice cold water
  • 1 ½ tbsp sugar

Instructions

For the Crust:

  1. Mix the flour and sugar until well combined.
  2. Mix in the cold butter until you achieve a breadcrumb consistency.
  3. Add the cold water and knead the dough until it comes together.
  4. Cover and refrigerate the dough for 30 minutes.

For the Filling:

  1. In a pot, add all the filling ingredients and boil until the liquid reduces.

For Assembly:

  1. Roll out the dough and cut out 10 small circles. If making mini Gizzadas, use a 3" cookie cutter to make 20.
  2. Pinch the edges of the dough to form a crust around the filling.
  3. Use a fork to poke holes in the bottom of each crust to ensure it cooks thoroughly.
  4. Pre-bake the crust at 350°F for 10 minutes to prevent the dough from being raw under the filling.
  5. Once the crust is pre-baked, add the filling and bake for another 20 minutes.

Sorrel Drink (Non-Alcoholic)

Sorrel drink is a traditional Jamaican Christmas beverage made from the Roselle plant, a variety of hibiscus. It is infused with spices such as ginger, cinnamon, and allspice, creating a refreshing and flavorful drink that is enjoyed chilled.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb (453.59 g) fresh sorrel
  • 5 oz (141.75 g) fresh ginger, thinly sliced
  • 10 pimento berries (allspice)
  • 1 cinnamon stick (or 3 cinnamon leaves)
  • 1 cup (0.24 l) white rum (optional, or add more for desired strength)
  • 1 ½ cups (300 g) sugar (sweeten to your taste)
  • 6 cups (1.89 l) water

Instructions

  1. Thoroughly wash the sorrel and discard the water.
  2. Place the washed sorrel in a stainless steel pot along with the ginger, pimento berries, and 6 cups of water.
  3. Bring to a boil and let it boil for 5-10 minutes. Then turn off the heat.
  4. Allow the mixture to steep for at least 12 hours or overnight to extract the full flavor.
  5. Strain the mixture well to remove the solids.
  6. Sweeten the sorrel drink to your taste with sugar and add rum if desired.
  7. Chill the sorrel drink and serve cold for a refreshing treat.
