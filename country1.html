Nigeria

From spicy street foods to hearty home-cooked meals, Nigerian cuisine is a bold celebration of flavor, culture, and tradition. Whether you're enjoying a crispy Akara in the morning or sharing Suya around a fire, food is central to connection and community in Nigeria.

Appetizers

Start your journey with flavorful Nigerian bites

🍽️ Akara (Black-Eyed Pea Fritters)

Fluffy inside, crispy outside — the perfect Nigerian breakfast or snack.

🧾 Ingredients

  • 2 cups dry black-eyed peas (peeled & soaked for 1–2 hrs)
  • 1 habanero pepper
  • 1 large onion (chopped)
  • 1 tsp salt or bouillon
  • ¾ cup water
  • 3 cups canola oil (for frying)

🧑🏾‍🍳 Instructions

  1. Clean, soak, and peel black-eyed peas. Soak for 1–2 hrs.
  2. Blend peas with pepper, onion, and water until smooth.
  3. Whisk batter with salt for 6 minutes to add air.
  4. Heat oil. Scoop batter into oil carefully and fry until golden brown, flipping once.
  5. Drain on paper towels and serve hot with pap or bread.
Tips:
  • Use a powerful blender for smooth texture.
  • Whisking is crucial for fluffiness.
  • Use the onion bubble test to check oil temp.

🔥 Beef Suya (Spicy Skewered Meat)

Spicy, smoky, and full of Hausa heritage flavor.

🧾 Ingredients

  • 2½ lbs beef (flank, skirt, or sirloin)
  • ½ – 1 tsp salt
  • ½ tsp bouillon or 1 cube
  • Cayenne pepper (optional)
  • ¾ cup suya spice (yaji) + extra for dusting
  • 1 tbsp peanut or vegetable oil

🧑🏾‍🍳 Instructions

  1. Soak skewers for 30 mins. Slice beef thinly and season.
  2. Marinate in fridge for 1 hour with oil, salt, bouillon.
  3. Skewer meat, coat with yaji, and grill at 400°F for 20 mins (flip halfway).
  4. Serve with tomato, onion, and cucumber slices.
Fun Fact:

Suya originated among the Hausa and is now a beloved West African street food.

🥟 Meat Pie (Savory Nigerian Pastry)

Flaky, buttery crust filled with a flavorful meat and veggie mix.

🧾 Ingredients

  • 500g minced beef
  • 700g plain flour + extra
  • 380g unsalted butter
  • 1 egg (for egg wash)
  • 4–8 tbsp water (100ml)
  • 1 beef bouillon cube
  • 1 tsp curry powder, 1 tsp thyme
  • 1 onion, 2 potatoes (cubed), 1 carrot (cubed)
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil, 1 tsp sugar (optional)

🧑🏾‍🍳 Instructions

  1. Mix flour, salt, butter, sugar to form crumbs. Add water to form dough. Chill 30 mins.
  2. Sauté onion in oil. Add beef, seasonings, and cook till browned.
  3. Add cooked potatoes, carrots, and a flour slurry to thicken. Cool filling.
  4. Roll out dough, cut into circles, fill, seal with egg, and fold.
  5. Egg wash tops, pierce with fork, and bake at 350°F for 20–30 mins.
Notes:
  • Cool filling completely before stuffing dough.
  • You can use salted butter but skip adding extra salt to dough.
  • Use a kitchen scale for best results!

Main Dishes

Delve into hearty Nigerian meals that capture the soul of our culture.

🍚 Jollof Rice

A beloved dish of West Africa, known for its rich and flavorful tomato base.

🧾 Ingredients

  • 1 large red bell pepper, cut into small pieces
  • 2 medium vine tomatoes, cut into small pieces
  • 1 red onion, quartered
  • 2 red scotch bonnet chillies, quartered
  • 3 garlic cloves, smashed
  • 25g fresh ginger, peeled and roughly chopped
  • 100ml water
  • 150ml vegetable oil
  • 1 red onion, finely chopped
  • 150g double concentrated tomato purée
  • 1 tbsp curry powder
  • 2 tsp dried thyme
  • 3 chicken stock cubes
  • 2 dried bay leaves
  • 600ml water
  • 600g white basmati rice

🧑🏾‍🍳 Instructions

  1. Place the base ingredients in a blender and blitz until smooth.
  2. Heat the vegetable oil in a large Dutch oven over medium heat. Add the onion and cook for 3 minutes.
  3. Add the tomato paste, blended base, thyme, curry powder, chicken stock, and bay leaves. Simmer for 10 minutes.
  4. Add rice and stir to coat. Pour in water, cover, and cook over low heat for 20-25 minutes until the rice is fully cooked.
  5. Serve hot with grilled chicken or fried plantains.
Fun Fact:

Jollof rice is a staple at Nigerian parties and has sparked an ongoing debate across West Africa about who makes the best version!

🍲 Efo Riro with Pounded Yam

A traditional spinach stew paired with the comforting texture of pounded yam.

🧾 Ingredients

  • 1 can of plum tomatoes
  • 1 red bell pepper (Tatashe)
  • 2 scotch bonnets
  • 1 onion
  • ¼ - ¾ cup chicken stock
  • Salt to taste
  • 1-2 chicken stock cubes
  • Pre-cooked assorted meats (beef, chicken, pomo, etc.)
  • 4 bunches of red root spinach
  • Palm oil

🧑🏾‍🍳 Instructions

  1. In a blender, blend the scotch bonnets, bell pepper, onion, and plum tomatoes until smooth.
  2. In a large saucepan, heat palm oil. Add iru, then pour in the blended mixture and bring to a boil.
  3. Stir in chicken stock, salt, and stock cubes. Add the assorted meats and cook for 5 minutes.
  4. Add spinach, stir, and cook for 10-15 minutes until the spinach is tender and well-combined with the sauce.
  5. Serve with pounded yam.

🍠 Pounded Yam Instructions

  • Peel yams and slice into ½-inch thick pieces.
  • Boil yams in a pot for 30 minutes until tender.
  • Place boiled yams into a food processor and blend until smooth and dough-like.
Tip:

Pounded yam is traditionally made by pounding boiled yam in a mortar and pestle for a smooth, stretchy texture!

🐟 Grilled Fish and Plantain

Enjoy the smokiness of grilled fish paired with the sweetness of plantain.

🧾 Ingredients

  • 1 whole tilapia
  • 2 large ripe plantains
  • 1 large onion, sliced
  • 2 scotch bonnets
  • 1 tbsp minced ginger
  • 1 tbsp minced garlic
  • 1 tbsp thyme
  • 1 tbsp rosemary
  • 1 tbsp fresh lemon/lime juice
  • 1 tbsp cayenne pepper
  • Seasoning cubes (Maggi/Knorr)
  • 1 red bell pepper
  • Salt to taste
  • Vegetable oil
  • Frying oil

🧑🏾‍🍳 Instructions

  1. Clean and make diagonal cuts in the fish. Blend cayenne, thyme, garlic, ginger, rosemary, and onions until smooth.
  2. Massage the marinade into the fish, cover, and refrigerate for 1-2 hours.
  3. Preheat the grill to 220°C/428°F. Place the fish on foil and grill for 30-40 minutes, flipping once halfway.
  4. Meanwhile, peel and slice the plantains. Heat oil in a frying pan and fry until golden brown.
  5. Serve the fish hot with fried plantains and enjoy!
Fun Fact:

This dish is perfect for outdoor gatherings, where grilled fish and plantain are enjoyed with loved ones.

Desserts and Drinks

End your meal with a sweet treat or refreshing drink to complement the flavors.

🍩 Puff Puff

Golden and crispy on the outside, fluffy on the inside, Puff Puff is a Nigerian street food favorite.

🧾 Ingredients

  • 600g all-purpose flour
  • 10g fast action yeast
  • 150g sugar (use as desired)
  • ½ tsp salt
  • 550-650g lukewarm water
  • Vegetable oil for frying

🧑🏾‍🍳 Instructions

  1. In a shallow bowl, add flour and salt together (combine salt with flour first).
  2. Add other dry ingredients and combine well.
  3. Add lukewarm water in bits to form a batter (the batter should not be runny and it shouldn't be thick either).
  4. Cover the bowl with cling film or a damp towel and place it in a warm place to proof until it doubles in size (usually between an hour or two).
  5. Once the mixture has risen, heat oil in a frying pan. Drop a tiny bit of batter to test if the oil is ready. If the batter floats to the top, it’s ready.
  6. Scoop the batter using your palm and drop it into the oil. Fry until golden brown and repeat the process.
Tip:

If the hand method isn’t for you, you can use a scoop to drop the batter into the oil.

🍹 Chapman

A colorful and refreshing drink that perfectly balances sweetness and tang.

🧾 Ingredients

  • 2 cups orange soda, preferably Fanta
  • 2 cups Sprite
  • ¼ cup grenadine syrup
  • Sliced orange
  • Sliced cucumber

🧑🏾‍🍳 Instructions

  1. Pour all ingredients into a pitcher and stir to combine.
  2. Serve over ice with slices of lemon and cucumber.
Fun Fact:

Chapman is often served at Nigerian parties and is a favorite drink during hot weather.
